Tajine Zitoune Recipe

Description

Tajine Zitoune is a cornerstone of Algerian festive cuisine, frequently served during Ramadan and special family gatherings. This elegant, aromatic dish features tender chicken pieces slow-simmered in a velvety broth flavored with onions, garlic, and a hint of cinnamon. The star of the dish—briny green olives—undergoes a specific blanching process to remove excess salt while retaining their distinct flavor, creating a perfect savory balance against the natural sweetness of sliced carrots.

Ingredients

For the Main Stew

  • 4-6 pieces Chicken (thighs, drumsticks, or whole quarters)
  • 1 large Onion, finely grated or chopped
  • 3 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 1-2 tbsp Smeg (clarified butter) or vegetable oil
  • 1 Bay leaf
  • Spices: 1/2 tbsp ginger, 1/2 tbsp turmeric or saffron threads, a pinch of cinnamon, salt, and black pepper

For the Olives & Aromatics

  • 300g Pitted green olives
  • 3-4 Carrots, peeled and sliced into rounds
  • 1 tbsp All-purpose flour (optional for thickening)
  • A few sprigs Fresh parsley, finely chopped (for garnish)
  • 1/2 tbsp Fresh lemon (for serving)
  • Approx. 500ml Hot water or chicken stock

Preparation

  1. Desalt the Olives
    2.

    Green olives are naturally very salty. To balance them, place the olives in a pot of cold water and bring to a boil for 5-10 minutes. Drain them, change the water, and repeat the process a second time. Alternatively, soak them in cold water with a tablespoon of flour and a slice of lemon for a few hours. Drain and set aside.

  2. Sear the Chicken & Aromatics
    3.

    In a heavy-bottomed pot or a traditional ceramic tajine, melt the smeg (or heat your oil) over medium heat. Add the chicken pieces and sear them for 5-7 minutes until they develop a light golden color. Add the grated onion and minced garlic, turning down the heat slightly so the garlic doesn't burn. Sauté until the onions are completely soft and translucent.

  3. Spice and Simmer
    4.

    Stir in the turmeric (or saffron), ginger, a pinch of cinnamon, black pepper, and the bay leaf. Go very easy on the salt at this stage, as the olives will still release some saltiness later. Toss the chicken to coat it beautifully in the spiced oil, then pour in enough hot water or stock to cover the chicken pieces halfway. Cover and simmer on low-medium heat for 20 minutes.

  4. Add Carrots and Olives
    5.

    Add the sliced carrots and the blanched green olives directly into the pot. Stir gently into the sauce. Cover the pot again and let everything simmer together on low heat for an additional 20-25 minutes, or until the chicken is completely tender and falling off the bone, and the carrots are fork-tender.

  5. Thicken the Sauce & Serve
    6.

    If your sauce is too thin, remove the lid for the final 5 minutes to let it reduce and thicken into a velvety gravy. (Optional: You can mix 1 tsp of flour with a splash of cold water and stir it into the boiling sauce to instantly bind it). Turn off the heat, stir in a handful of fresh chopped parsley, and serve hot with a squeeze of fresh lemon juice and plenty of crusty bread.
